About Me

I am currently a Software Developer at General Motors, where I work on the Advanced Vehicle Inventory Platform. While working at General Motors, I have had the opportunity to work on various projects that have increased the company's profits and cut costs. I upgraded a monolithic Java application to a microservice architecture using spring boot as the main framework for the majority of my work. ElasticSearch was the main database used due to the needs for optimal search performance, given the millions of queries per day hitting the various vehicle inventory search endpoints. However, after recently upgrading the vehicle ingestion pipeline, PostGresSQL is also used with functions and tables to prioritize and aggregate data. The main method of triggering ingestion of a vehicle and initiating the various enhancement microservices is through Kafka driven events. Based on various business defined criteria, these vehicles could take a very different path of enhancement based on: vehicle condition, country, zip code, dealership, make, model, and year. I utilized Kubernetes/Azure to manage and orchestrate the deployments and scale the pods as needed. I have regularly participated in critical application support calls, and have found numerous resolutions to bugs that have resolved clients issues. One of my favorite things to do is discover the root cause of a problem or bug in order to provide an optimal solution.
